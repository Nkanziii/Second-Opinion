The increasing use of Artificial intelligence and machine learning imbedded in healthcare professional systems. 

My research question what i wanna explore
### "How does witnessing two AI systems visibly disagree - through both voices and generative visual form - affect a person's ability to calibrate trust in AI generated healthcare advice?"

Not a can we trust Ai more so how much can we rely on AI as healthcare professionals?

The live process of calibrating trust through user interaction with my ai models - "How does the  user start to feel in moments of disagreement, between the models?"


-> My piece isnt just about Ai being wrong its a deeper dive into, more so about what happens to a person's judgment when confident sounding authorities contradict eachother in the stakes of life and death. Over trusting systems is its own failure mode.

In nhs article - Ai is increasingly embedded across diagnostics, treatment planning, and patient monitoring worldwide, but it flags data privacy, algorithmic bias, lack of interpretability (often can't tell why ai reached a conclusion), weak regulation, and the need to keep a human clinician in the loop rather than letting AI act alone.

even a few incorrect, overconfident AI predictions damage user trust substantially, with very slow recovery — and importantly, confidently-wrong and hesitantly-right miscalibrations damage trust in different ways. That's a nice academic anchor for your "confident vs. cautious" clinician asymmetry
